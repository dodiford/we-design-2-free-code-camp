KOFI ANNAN

The first Sub-Saharan African UN Secretary-General


Here's a time-line of Kofi Annan's life:


  • 1938: Kofi Annan was born in Kumasi, Ghana.

    • Early Life and Education


  • Kofi Annan was born into an elite Ghanaian Family.His father was a provincial governor and a member of the influential Fante ethnic group.

  • He attended the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology in Ghana before completing his undergraduate studies in Economic at Macalester College in Minnesota,USA.

  • He later earned a Mater's degree bin International Affairs from the Graduate Institute Geneva in Switzerland.

    • Career at the United Nations


  • Kofi Annan began his career at the United Nations (UN) in 1962, working for the world Health Organization's Geneva office

  • Kofi Annan became the 7th Secretary-General of the UN and the first person from sub-Saharan Afric to hold this prestigious position.

    • Key Achievements


  • Nobel peace Prize(2001):Annan and the UN were jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their work for a better organized and more peaceful world.

  • Millennium Development Goals (MDGs): He championed the creation of the MDGs, a global effort to reduce extreme poverty, improve health, and promote education and equality.

  • Global Compact Initiative:Launched by Annan in 1999, the UN Global Compact encouraged businesses worldwide to adopt sustainable and socially responsible policies.

    • Later Years and Legacy

  • After leaving the UN, Annan continued his work as a global statesman, serving as a special envoy to Syria during the civil war and leading the Kofi Annan Foundation, which promotes peace, sustainable development, and human rights.

  • He is remembered as a diplomatic leader, peacemaker, and a defender of human dignity

    • “The world is not ours to keep. We hold it in trust for future generations.”-Kofi Annan
